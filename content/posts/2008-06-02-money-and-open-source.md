<a href="http://day-to-day-stuff.blogspot.com/">Erik van Oosten</a> wrote a post on Open Source and money: "<a href="http://day-to-day-stuff.blogspot.com/2008/06/do-not-offer-money.html">Do Not Offer Money</a>".  He quotes my post about <a href="http://www.ericdaugherty.com/blog/2008/01/java-email-server-20-branch.html">experience with JES 2.0 and the JBoss Email server</a>.  It is an interesting topic and he provides a nice anecdote about Christmas dinner.

The point is well made though.  There are different 'modes' and expectations when you are doing it for 'fun (insert any non-financial motivation)' versus for pay.

I also want to be careful to not overstate the switch.  I've continued some work on JES as well as other projects since then (<a href="http://www.ericdaugherty.com/dev/itunesexport">iTunesExport</a>).  But the immediate impact on THAT project (JES 2) was significant.

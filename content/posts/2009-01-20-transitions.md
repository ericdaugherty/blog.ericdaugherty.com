Today is a day of transitions.  I won't get into the politics of it, but from a technology side, the new administration is up and running.<br /><br /><a href="http://www.whitehouse.gov/blog/">A blog</a>! (<a href="http://www.whitehouse.gov/feed/blog.aspx">rss</a>)  President Obama, or at least his administration is already up and blogging on the Whitehouse website.<br /><br />The details...<br /><ul><li>RSS - The rss feed is summary only, not full text.  You will have to click through for details.</li><li>.Net - The website uses .Net.  While the main URLs (correctly) keep the aspx extension out, the RSS feeds all have the .aspx extension.  You can also tell by the tag ids: 'ctl09_rptNavigation_ctl02_rptNavigationItems_ctl01_hlSubNav'</li><li>JQuery - It looks like the Whitehouse site is using the JQuery JavaScript libraries</li><li>Webtrends - The government is watching you...</li></ul>We'll see how the blog gets used over time.  I will be disappointed if it is simply a channel for the press releases but I don't expect the President to be blogging or twittering constantly either.  I think he'll stay plenty busy.

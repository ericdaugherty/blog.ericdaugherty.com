Two years ago I wrote a blog post about <a href="http://blog.ericdaugherty.com/2010/07/hiking-with-dslr-camera.html">Hiking with a DSLR</a>. &nbsp;I had just recently moved to Colorado and was enjoying all the great hikes but struggling with how to take my camera with me in an unobtrusive but accessible manner. &nbsp;Since then I've tried a few new options and thought I would write an update.

My camera is a still the Nikon D300, and my primary hiking lens is the Nikkor 17-55 2.8.

First, I supported the <a href="http://www.kickstarter.com/projects/97103764/capture-camera-clip-system">Capture Kickstarter</a> project. &nbsp;I ended up purchasing two of them and found they were a pretty good solution for smaller cameras. &nbsp;However, with my D300 and the 17-55 2.8 the camera and lens were just a bit too heavy for longer hikes. &nbsp;I ended up giving these to my Dad, who has a D40 with some reasonably sized lenses and it works great. &nbsp;I also like the Capture because the camera mount is Arca-Swiss compatible, so you can keep a single bracket on your camera and switch between the Capture and a tripod. &nbsp;The kickstarter is long over but you can find them at <a href="http://peakdesignltd.com/">peakdesignltd.com</a>

The original Cotton Carrier (vest) was an good solution, but the chest harness was pretty bulky. &nbsp;I certainly used it quite a bit, but something smaller and less obtrusive was appealing. &nbsp;I bought the <a href="http://buy.cottoncarrier.com/category-s/44.htm">holster</a>&nbsp;and liked it, but it was better for casual walks than hiking. &nbsp;You really don't want something bumping against your legs on a long hike.

I'd also found that over time I was slightly annoyed by the solution I came up with to put the Cotton Carrier hub on the camera. &nbsp;Here is what <a href="http://blog.ericdaugherty.com/2010/07/hiking-with-dslr-camera.html">I wrote before</a>:

<blockquote class="tr_bq">
I looked into using quick release plates with my Cotton Carrier and realized all I needed was an additional clamp. &nbsp;You simply screw the Cotton Carrier round attachment to the Clamp, and then you simply clamp the clamp to the camera. &nbsp;This approach increases the distance from the camera to the carrier a little, but I didn't find it to be a problem.</blockquote>
Since then, Cotton Carrier came out with a newer solution, the <a href="http://buy.cottoncarrier.com/cotton-carrier-strapshot-p/211cps.htm">StrapShot</a>. &nbsp;They were nice enough to send me one, and I was intrigued. &nbsp;It is essentially a&nbsp;competitor&nbsp;to the Capture, but uses the Cotton Carrier hub solution instead of the Capture locking system. &nbsp;You simply mount the StrapShot on any strap (I put it on my day pack's shoulder strap) and then put the camera on it. &nbsp;It comes with safety strap and a hand strap, which I really liked and now is now my main 'camera strap' for casual use.

However, I still wanted an Arca-Swiss plate on my camera full time, and the use of the clamp/Cotton Carrier Hub was just bulky enough to turn me off. &nbsp;But Cotton also has '<a href="http://buy.cottoncarrier.com/Cotton-Carrier-UNIVERSAL-ADAPTER-PLATE-p/766nhp.htm">Universal Adapter Plate</a>' that mounts to your camera and provides Arca-Swiss style mounting with a Cotton Carrier Hub. &nbsp;This removed much of the bulk in mounting the camera and has become my&nbsp;permanently&nbsp;installed camera bracket.

So I am pretty happy at this point with the <a href="http://buy.cottoncarrier.com/cotton-carrier-strapshot-p/211cps.htm">StrapShot</a> + <a href="http://buy.cottoncarrier.com/Cotton-Carrier-UNIVERSAL-ADAPTER-PLATE-p/766nhp.htm">Universal Adapter Plate</a> solution. &nbsp;It allows me to comfortably carry my camera while I hike, keep the camera Tripod ready, and still use the holster or my <a href="http://www.blackrapid.com/">Rapid Strap</a>&nbsp;for more casual outings.

I'm sure that there will be even better solutions in another two years, but for now I feel that I have a very solid solution.

Note: I purchased two Captures, the Cotton Carrier Vest and Holders, and various adapters myself, but Cotton did provide me with the StrapShot and Universal Adapter plate.

<i>Update: I've posted an updated version of this here: <a href="http://blog.ericdaugherty.com/2012/10/hiking-with-dslr-part-2.html">Hiking with a DSLR Part 2</a>.</i><br />
<br />
I just spent a week in Rocky Mountain National Park hiking with my family.  It was a great week, and it allowed me to give my new hiking setup a full workout.<br />
<br />
My camera is a Nikon D300, and my primary hiking lens is the Nikkor  17-55 2.8.&nbsp; Since I have small kids, I carry a child carrier, either  a Kelty <a href="http://www.kelty.com/p-281-fc-30.aspx">FC3</a> or <a href="http://www.kelty.com/p-383-fc-20.aspx">FC1</a>.&nbsp; None of my normal carrying solutions (outlined below) worked for hiking, so I needed something different.<br />
<br />
I came across the <a href="http://www.cottoncarrier.com/">Cotton Carrier</a>, which is a chest harness for the camera.&nbsp; You screw a small round attachment into the bottom of your camera, and it slides into the chest harness.&nbsp; It also provides a Velcro strap to immobilize the camera further, although I only use this if I need to scramble up rocks or jog.&nbsp; It also provides a safety strap that I attach to where a normal camera strap would attach.<br />
<br />
In this setup, I don't use a camera strap at all.&nbsp; Between the harness and the safety strap, the camera isn't going anywhere, and it provides for clean access to the camera.<br />
<br />
When I first got my carrier, it didn't come with the safety strap, and I kept a very short camera strap on the camera to provide for a little extra safety when I had it out of the carrier.&nbsp; When they added this feature they offered it for free (plus postage) which was a nice touch.<br />
<br />
This setup worked pretty well, but it was frustrating to use the Tripod which I sometimes carry with me on hikes.&nbsp; To address this, I moved to a solution where I 'always' used a quick-release solution.<br />
<br />
I use Arca Swiss compatible plates and clamps.&nbsp; Initially, I had a generic plate for my D300 and a Kirk <a href="http://www.kirkphoto.com/2_in_Quick_Release_Clamp.html">QRC-2</a> clamp on my tripod.&nbsp; This worked alright, but the plate was bulky so I always took it off the camera when I wasn't using it, which was time consuming.<br />
<br />
I looked into using quick release plates with my Cotton Carrier and realized all I needed was an additional clamp.&nbsp; You simply screw the Cotton Carrier round attachment to the Clamp, and then you simply clamp the clamp to the camera.&nbsp; This approach increases the distance from the camera to the carrier a little, but I didn't find it to be a problem.<br />
<br />
I also upgraded my generic plate to a Kirk plate made specifically for the D300, the Kirk <a href="http://www.kirkphoto.com/Camera_Plate_for_Nikon_D300S.html">PZ-122</a>.&nbsp; This plate was MUCH better than the generic plate I used before.&nbsp; It is much more low profile, and has a 'stopper' you can put on one side to the plates can only go on and off on one side.&nbsp; Additionally, it has a second screw hole, so you can attach the camera to other things that use this attachment without removing the plate.&nbsp; Since I regularly use a Rapid Strap, that screws into that hole, it turned out to be a great feature.<br />
<br />
All in all, I'm very happy with the Cotton Carrier + Kirk Plates/Clamp system in general, and for Hiking in particular.<br />
<br />
The harness looks like it covers your entire chest, but only the bottom band (about 1 1/2 inches) is really snug against your chest.&nbsp; It does get a little sweaty, but not bad considering, and nothing compared to my hiking backpack.<br />
<br />
When I'm not hiking, I use one of the following (all of which I like for specific uses):<br />
<ul>
<li> <a href="http://products.lowepro.com/product/SlingShot-200-AW,2035,4.htm">Lowepro SlingShot 200 AW</a></li>
<li><a href="http://www.blackrapid.com/product/camera-strap/rs-7/">BlackRapid R Strap</a></li>
<li>Traditional Camera Strap</li>
</ul>
The SlingShot is a great 'all day' bag for most outings.&nbsp; The 200 can carry my D300 with the 17-55 lens attached in the main hold, with two other (modestly sized) lenses.&nbsp; I also carry an extra battery, data card, the R Strap, Filters, and other miscellaneous gear I may need throughout the day.&nbsp; It provides easy access to the camera without taking the pack off, and is reasonably light.<br />
<br />
The R Strap works great when you are shooting a lot with the same lens and a 'casual' level of movement.&nbsp; It is not good for walking on non-level ground, walking long distances, or doing a lot of squatting.<br />
<br />
I rarely use the camera with a normal camera strap, but it is the traditional approach.

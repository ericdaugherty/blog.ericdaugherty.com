Every year or so I post about my camera setup, and the gear I use to carry it while hiking, etc. &nbsp;Here is this year's update...<br />
<br />
You can go back and read <a href="http://blog.ericdaugherty.com/2012/10/hiking-with-dslr-part-2.html">last year's post</a>, or my <a href="http://blog.ericdaugherty.com/2010/07/hiking-with-dslr-camera.html">2010 post</a> to see how I've changed over time.<br />
<br />
Since last year, I've upgraded from the Nikon D300 to the D600. &nbsp;Since it is now a full frame, I also switched my primary lens to the D600&nbsp;24-85mm f/3.5-4.5 kit lens. &nbsp;Both the D600 body and the lens are smaller than D300/17-55 f2.8 I carried before, although I did give up some speed on my walk around lens.<br />
<h3>
Camera Mount</h3>
The <a href="http://buy.cottoncarrier.com/Cotton-Carrier-UNIVERSAL-ADAPTER-PLATE-p/766nhp.htm">Cotton Universal Adapter</a> is constantly attached to my camera. &nbsp;With its&nbsp;Arca-Swiss compatibility, it can easily be mounted on my Tri-Pod and Mono-Pod, as well as carried using the Cotton system. &nbsp;I also have the <a href="http://buy.cottoncarrier.com/cotton-carrier-hand-strap-p/801chs.htm">Cotton Hand Strap</a> attached as my primary strap. &nbsp;I don't like or use a neck strap.<br />
<h3>
Hiking/Walking</h3>
I continue to find the <a href="http://buy.cottoncarrier.com/cotton-carrier-strapshot-p/211cps.htm">Cotton Strap Shot</a> to be the ideal carrier solution while hiking. &nbsp;Mine never leaves my REI daypack, which I carry on just about every day hike. &nbsp;It holds the camera securely and out of the way while hiking, but I can still have it up and ready to shoot in seconds. &nbsp;The tether is a nice bit of extra security as I don't keep a neck strap on my camera.<br />
<br />
While I own the <a href="http://buy.cottoncarrier.com/cotton-carrier-camera-vest-p/635rtl-s.htm">Cotton Carrier Vest</a> and <a href="http://buy.cottoncarrier.com/cotton-carrier-side-holster-p/501hsl.htm">Side Holster</a>, they don't see a ton of activity. &nbsp;The vest is great if you want a really stable mount for your DSLR, but I find that for most activities where it would make sense (biking, skiing), I carry my GoPro instead. &nbsp;I do occasionally use the side holster, and it is a good companion to a waist pack, where you have a heavier belt meant to support weight of it. &nbsp;I used it once this year on a shorter hike where I just wore a waist pack and it worked well.<br />
<br />
For more casual walks, I use my <a href="http://www.blackrapid.com/products/classic">BlackRapid strap</a>. &nbsp;It screws right into the Cotton Universal Adapter so it is quick to throw on the camera and works great for casual walks where you are taking a lot of pictures. &nbsp;It isn't stable enough for more intense walks or hiking, but if you are walking around town it is a great option. &nbsp;I've had my RS-1 since 2008, and it continues to perform well. &nbsp;Well worth the $44 dollars I spent, although it looks to be a bit more expensive now.<br />
<h3>
Tri/Mono Pods</h3>
I have a Gitzo Basalt Tripod (GT2932) that continues to work very well, and a&nbsp;Sirui P-326 Monopod that I like as well. &nbsp;The Sirui's padding is starting to split a little after only 6 months, but it is fine otherwise. &nbsp;Both with Arca-Swiss compatible mounts.<br />
<h3>
GoPro</h3>
In addition to the DSLR, I added a GoPro Hero3 to the mix this year as well. &nbsp;I primarily use it while skiing, although I have used it while swimming and snorkeling in the ocean this year as well. &nbsp;For carrying the GoPro, I use three main options:<br />
<br />
The&nbsp;<a href="http://gopro.com/camera-mounts/chest-mount-harness">GoPro Chesty</a>&nbsp;works great if you are primarily shooting video of yourself skiing. &nbsp;It provides a very stable platform (your torso), and makes the camera accessible to you, allowing you to control it directly instead of using a WiFi control (iPhone or Remote).<br />
<br />
The <a href="http://gopro.com/camera-mounts/helmet-front-mount">Helmet Mount</a>&nbsp;works well if you are taking video of someone else skiing. &nbsp;It allows you to steadily track them while you move back and forth across the mountain. &nbsp;It is more difficult to control, and pairs well with a WiFi control, although this really drains the battery, especially in cold conditions.<br />
<br />
I like both of the above and end up switching back and forth during the season. &nbsp;Either is a solid option.<br />
<br />
This summer I picked up the <a href="http://gopro.com/camera-mounts/hero3-wrist-housing">Wrist Housing</a>, primarily for my trip to Hawaii for use while swimming/snorkeling. &nbsp;I found it worked well. &nbsp;It was not overly obtrusive while swimming, and easy to control, even underwater. &nbsp;I also used it on a kayak trip, and again, it didn't bother me while paddling and kept the GoPro available for nearly every opportunity.<br />
<h3>
Bags</h3>
My main camera bag is the Lowepro SlingShot 200. &nbsp;I've used this bag for about 4 years now, and it is my primary carry bag. &nbsp;It fits the D600 with the 17-55 mounted, as well as my 80-200 and a couple prime lenses. &nbsp;This is really my main carry solution, as it works well to carry the DSLR when you want to have your other lenses available as well. &nbsp;You can quickly rotate the back from your back to your chest and pull out the camera. &nbsp;It also provides a steady platform for changing lenses on the move. &nbsp;It looks like the <a href="http://store.lowepro.com/sling-bags/slingshot-202-aw">Slingshot 202</a> is the current iteration of this bag.<br />
<br />
I also added a travel bag, the Fastpack 350. &nbsp;This allows me to carry my camera, all the lenses, as well as my laptop and assorted cables. &nbsp;This is my carry on bag when flying. &nbsp;Although I often throw my SlingShot in my checked luggage if I'm traveling for more than a weekend, so I can use it once I've arrived.<br />
<h3>
Summary</h3>
Overall, I'm very pleased with the solutions I have in place. &nbsp;I'm not looking to replace any of the solutions I have now, although I'm sure as new products come out I will be tempted.<br />
<br />
Note: I purchased almost everything myself, including the Cotton Carrier Vest, SideHolster and various adapters, but Cotton did provide me with the StrapShot and Universal Adapter plate for free.
